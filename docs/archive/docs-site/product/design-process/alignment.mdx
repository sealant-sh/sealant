Alignment is the stage where the open questions get answered out loud and written down, so the final
plan is built on a shared record rather than assumptions. This page is that "same page" record: the
decisions reached by working through every open question together. Five rounds of questions produced
seventeen decisions — and one surprise that reshaped everything else.

<Callout type="idea">
  The reframing that drove all the rest: **Sealant is not a SaaS and not a revenue business — it is
  an open-source, self-hosted adoption play.** Success is measured in adoption, developer mindshare,
  and community, not revenue. Every downstream decision follows from that.
</Callout>

## Identity and goal

What Sealant is for, and what "winning" means.

| Decision                | Answer                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| What success means      | Adoption, developer mindshare, community, and the founder's standing in the space. Not revenue.                                                                                                                          |
| Business / monetization | None, and not mentioned anywhere. Hosted "one day" is possible, but it appears in no positioning. This is a product and adoption plan, not a monetization plan.                                                          |
| Deployment              | Self-hosted only. No managed cloud. Remove "Sealant Cloud" and all hosted / two-sided SaaS framing from the story.                                                                                                       |
| Who it is for           | Indie and OSS developers, and builders of AI coding products — the star-givers, tinkerers, and people who need a recorded runtime they control. Not enterprise, regulated, or internal-platform as the primary audience. |
| License model           | Open-source. Open-core in spirit — everything OSS — with no paid tier today.                                                                                                                                             |

## The product shape

The decisions that define the v1 surface and where the lines of honesty are drawn.

<Cards>
  <Card title="v1 hero">
    The live environment plus the harness run. Give an agent a real workspace, run its harness, get
    a result. The execution record is the payoff, not the lead.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Public SDK surface">
    The fluent high-level SDK. The low-level runtime client becomes internal plumbing, and the REST
    create contract is reshaped to drop registry and tag friction.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Harness model">
    Both modes — one-shot (CI-style) and interactive on a long-lived workspace you can SSH into
    between tasks. Bring-your-own-harness stays a core principle.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Execution record grade">
    Dev-grade now, audit-grade later. Honest "evidence, not proof" for developers today; privilege
    separation, crypto digests, and an out-of-namespace spool sequenced as a later tier.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Browser support (v1)">
    Headless, harness-driven, captured as evidence. The agent drives a real in-workspace Chromium
    via CDP; screenshots, HAR, DOM, and navigations land in the run record.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Hosting target (v1)">
    Local-first with Docker. k8s and k3s come later. This matches code reality: Docker works, the
    k8s adapters are stubs.
  </Card>

### The fluent SDK surface

The public surface is the fluent, high-level SDK: create a workspace, run a harness against it, and
receive a structured result.

```ts
const { result, changes, artifacts, record } = await sealant.workspaces
  .create(/* ... */)
  .harness.run();
```

The browser story is deliberately not greenfield — it rides the existing `openForward` channel,
artifact store, and egress proxy, keeping it composable with the rest of the runtime.

### Tenancy and multi-tenant auth

Multi-tenant identity is explicitly **not Sealant's problem**. Self-hosted means one operator per
deployment, inside their own trusted perimeter. There are no orgs, teams, or quotas to build;
control-plane auth is the self-hoster's concern, not a multi-tenant identity system Sealant has to
ship.

## Platform and products split

Sealant separates the runtime platform from the products built on top of it. The split already
exists physically in the code (IPC-only, with no vendored `.proto`).

| Decision               | Answer                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| ---------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| The split              | Platform = "Sealant" — the `sealantd` runtime, the `.proto`, and the public SDK — as one project. Products are separate repos, each consuming the published SDK.                                         |
| Product branding       | Products get their own names and a "by Sealant" tag, plus their own identity — not "Sealant X" under an umbrella. The "Sealant Verify / Repro / Handoff" umbrella naming is dropped.                     |
| First flagship product | Handoff (task to verified change and PR), framed as the proof-of-platform reference that dogfoods the SDK end to end — not a revenue product. It is the heaviest to build, but it proves the whole loop. |
| Product family         | Handoff is first. Verify (behavior to proof to test) and Repro (report to runnable case) are committed roadmap, each its own "by Sealant" repo.                                                          |
| OSS scope              | Everything OSS. The platform is one repo; each product is its own repo on the public SDK. Dogfooding from outside proves the SDK is real.                                                                |

<Callout type="info">
  Dogfooding from outside is the point. Because each product consumes the same published SDK an
  external developer would, building them is itself the proof that the platform surface is real and
  complete.
</Callout>

## Adoption wedge

How a newcomer reaches a shareable moment in the first five minutes.

| Decision           | Answer                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| The 5-minute "wow" | The one-liner: create workspace → run harness → replay the recorded run. Fluent SDK DX plus the execution record is the hero demo, and it is the lightest path to a shareable moment.                |
| Marketing page     | Show multiple examples of the one-liner to demonstrate versatility — different harnesses for different jobs: coding agent, autonomous QA, bug repro, CI investigation, dependency updates, and more. |

## What this means for the narrative

The deltas from today's story. These are the concrete changes the final plan and the public surface
have to absorb.

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Cut the cloud Remove the "Sealant Cloud" tier, "join early access," and all managed, hosted,
    and two-sided language. The only deployment story is self-hosted, open-source.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Cut the monetization subtext No pricing, no enterprise, no "book a demo." The CTAs are star
    the repo, read the docs, and build on it.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Reconcile the README Retire the "workspaces plus issue workflows" two-loop contract. Adopt
    workspace, run, and harness as the primary nouns, with issue-to-PR demoted to a product
    (Handoff, by Sealant).
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Make the fluent SDK real or stop showing it The hero `workspaces.create(...).harness.run()`
    must exist as the public SDK — it does not today. This is the single most important build.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Make the record consumable The execution record is the payoff of every demo, but its read
    side is unbuilt: folds throw `Unimplemented` and inspector pages redirect to JSON. The replay
    experience is what makes the demo shareable, so it has to work.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Honesty as a feature For an OSS and adoption audience, the honest-boundaries posture is a
    trust-builder, not a weakness. Keep it explicit: "evidence, not proof"; "the tool reports, it
    does not judge"; tamper-evident, not tamper-proof.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    ### Brand the products as standalone Give Handoff, Verify, and Repro their own names and repos.
    The "by Sealant" tag signals they are proof the platform is real.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Open context

<Accordions>
  <Accordion title="Why drop revenue and SaaS framing entirely?">
    The defining decision of this stage was that success means adoption, mindshare, and community
    standing rather than revenue. A hosted offering may exist "one day," but it does not appear in
    any positioning. Treating the plan as a product and adoption play — not a monetization plan — is
    what justifies removing the cloud tier, the pricing, and the two-sided SaaS language across the
    board.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Why is the execution record dev-grade, not audit-grade, at v1?">
    The record is honest about its boundaries: it is evidence, not proof — tamper-evident and
    best-effort for developers today. The stronger guarantees (privilege separation, cryptographic
    digests, an out-of-namespace spool) are real and sequenced as a later tier, not abandoned.
    Stating that boundary plainly is treated as a trust-builder for an OSS audience.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Why ship Handoff first if it is the heaviest product?">
    Handoff (task to verified change and PR) exercises the entire platform loop end to end, so it
    doubles as the proof-of-platform reference that dogfoods the SDK from outside. It is the
    heaviest to build, but proving the whole loop is exactly what makes the platform credible.
    Verify and Repro follow as committed roadmap, each its own "by Sealant" repo.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
