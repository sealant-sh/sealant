Evidence Review — Design System

The visual language of Sealant’s evidence-backed review surface — shared by the web app, the marketing site, and @sealant/ui. Warm and premium, with real depth: the tokens, the three-voice type split, the four-color discipline, the lifted components, and the rules that keep it a review tool, not a dashboard.

Surface: desktop 1440 × 900Space Grotesk · Inter · JetBrains Mono4 semantic accentsv3 · depth & polish

00Principles

Evidence beside code

A reviewer never carries a number from one page to verify it on another. The change and its proof share one viewport.

The tool reports

It shows observations, never verdicts. No confidence scores, no “safe to merge.” The reviewer judges.

Hierarchy without containers

Structure comes from spacing, alignment, and thin rules — not cards, pills, or chips.

Color is earned

Each accent marks one specific meaning. No panel is ever flooded with it.

06What the system refuses

An equal part of the system is what it never renders. Each was a live option, rejected.

✕No panel tinted green, amber, or red. Color marks a value or a word — it never floods a region, because a tinted panel renders a verdict.
✕No more than one prominent warning. Two warnings of equal weight means neither is the one that matters.
✕No vanity metric tiles, no pills or chips as containers. Depth and type carry hierarchy — though real cards that group content are fine.
✕No confidence score or risk dashboard. A number invents precision the evidence does not have.
✕No glassmorphism, neon, or heavy drop shadows. Depth is one restrained soft shadow — never a hard, blurry, or glassy one.
✕No banned register. strong proof, high confidence, all checks passed, safe to merge — each substitutes the tool’s verdict for the reviewer’s.

Every token and component above reduces to one rule: show the reviewer the code and its proof in the same place, in plain language, and let them decide.
